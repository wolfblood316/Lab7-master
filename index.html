The Siege of Godesberg, 18 November – 17 December 1583, was the first major siege of the Cologne War (1583–89). A formidable 13th-century fortress, the Godesburg (pictured c. 1500), sat on top of the Godesberg mountain, towering over the Rhine valley. It commanded the roads leading to Bonn, the Elector of Cologne's capital city, and Cologne, the region's economic powerhouse. By the mid-16th century
